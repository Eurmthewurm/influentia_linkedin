Influentia vs Clay

Clay is a data enrichment and workflow platform. Influentia is an outreach autopilot. They're solving adjacent problems, and depending on your stage, you might want one, the other, or both. Here's an honest breakdown.

TL;DR
  • Clay enriches leads from many data sources and can trigger sequences. It's powerful but requires setup time and starts at $149/month (meaningfully more for real usage).
  • Influentia finds leads, writes messages, and follows up — in one click. Zero setup beyond describing your ideal customer. $97/month flat.
  • Clay is built for sales teams with a dedicated person to run it. Influentia is built for founders and consultants doing their own outreach.
  • Clay doesn't run the outreach itself — you still need a sending tool. Influentia does everything end-to-end including sending via LinkedIn directly.
  • Clay has no Reddit. Influentia scans Reddit daily for buyer intent signals.

Clay is a platform. Influentia is a product.

Clay's power is its flexibility. You can pull data from 100+ sources, run AI research on each row, build complex multi-step workflows, and push results to any tool in your stack. If you have the time and knowledge to build those workflows — and a team to maintain them — Clay is genuinely impressive.

The tradeoff is that Clay is a blank canvas. It doesn't find your leads, it doesn't know your ICP by default, and it doesn't send anything on its own — you need to connect it to a separate sending tool (like Smartlead, Instantly, or Expandi). You're assembling a pipeline, not clicking a button.

Influentia makes different assumptions: you're a founder or consultant doing your own outreach, you don't want to build infrastructure, and you want to describe your ideal customer once and have the tool run from there. Setup is ten minutes. There's nothing to connect.

The cost reality

Clay's starter plan is $149/month but it's credit-based. Every enrichment row consumes credits. A list of 500 well-enriched leads can easily eat $200–$500 in credits before you've sent a single message. Real usage for a founder doing 200–300 outreach per month lands at $300–$600/month when you include the sending tool.

Influentia is $97/month with no per-lead or per-message fees. Unlimited leads, unlimited messages (within LinkedIn's conservative daily caps, which are intentional — not a limitation, a safety feature).

When Clay makes more sense

Clay wins if you have a dedicated person to build and maintain the workflow, you're doing high-volume outreach across email and LinkedIn simultaneously, you need multi-source data enrichment beyond what a web search provides, or you want to build something highly custom that doesn't exist as a product.

A Series A sales team with an SDR who lives in Clay is the right use case. A solo founder who wants to spend 10 minutes a day on outreach is not.

Bottom line

Clay is the right tool for teams that want to build a customised data pipeline. Influentia is the right tool for individuals who want the outcome — qualified conversations — without building the pipeline to get there. If you're not sure which you are: if you've never used Clay before and you sell professional services, start with Influentia.
