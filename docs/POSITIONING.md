# Influentia — Positioning, ICP, and the Ecosystem Flywheel
_Who we sell to, who we don't, what we say, and how Influentia and Authentik Studio reinforce each other — without merging brands._

**Status:** locked decisions for the 1.0 relaunch.
**Read this before:** writing landing copy, designing onboarding, naming a feature, picking an ad audience, replying to "is this for me?" emails.

**Brand architecture rule (read first):** Influentia is its own brand and stands alone. Authentik Studio is a separate business that uses Influentia internally and refers high-touch customers from Influentia to itself. They reinforce each other through Ermo as the human bridge and through content — *not* through merged identity. Full reasoning in [`BLUEPRINT.md`](./BLUEPRINT.md) §2.

---

## 1. The product in one sentence (outcome-led, not feature-led)

> **Influentia gives B2B founders a daily pipeline of warm sales conversations — without hiring an SDR, without learning a sales tool, and without a single message leaving their machine.**

Test it: read that to a stranger. They should be able to say "ah, it's like having a remote SDR who works on my laptop." If they can't, the sentence is wrong.

What the sentence is *deliberately* not:
- Not "LinkedIn automation" (category-level, generic, fights Expandi on features).
- Not "AI outreach tool" (saturated, signals 2024).
- Not "lead generation software" (sounds like Apollo).
- Not "personal brand growth" (that's the Authentik agency, not the product).

The wedge is **outcome + how**: a daily pipeline (outcome) without an SDR / without a sales tool / without your data leaving your machine (how).

## 2. ICP — three personas, sharply defined

Every Influentia customer fits one of these three. If a prospect doesn't fit, **decline politely.** Bad-fit customers cost more than they pay.

### 2.1 The Bootstrapped B2B SaaS Founder
- **Stage:** $1k–$10k MRR, 0–3 employees, post-product-market-fit-search, pre-Series A.
- **Pain:** "I'm the salesperson. I hate it. I'm bad at it. It eats my evenings."
- **Goal:** 3–8 qualified discovery calls per week without becoming a salesperson.
- **Channels they're already on:** LinkedIn (light), Twitter, Reddit (quietly).
- **Buying trigger:** Just hit a churn cliff or just lost a big customer.
- **Objection:** "I tried Apollo / Lemlist and it felt spammy."
- **What wins them:** Local-first privacy + AI personalisation that actually reads the prospect's posts.

### 2.2 The B2B Agency Owner (10–50 employees)
- **Stage:** $50k–$500k MRR, scaling, hates being the rainmaker.
- **Pain:** "All my pipeline depends on referrals or my own LinkedIn. I can't grow past my personal bandwidth."
- **Goal:** Inbound flow from LinkedIn + a way to nurture warm contacts without chasing them.
- **Channels:** LinkedIn (heavy), email, occasional events.
- **Buying trigger:** Lost a major retainer, or hit a quarterly target ceiling.
- **Objection:** "My team already uses [Expandi/HubSpot/Pipedrive]."
- **What wins them:** The tool that **doesn't** require their team to learn anything — they run it themselves, then hand the warm leads to the team.

### 2.3 The B2B Consultant / Fractional Exec
- **Stage:** $5k–$50k engagements, 2–8 active clients, always 1–2 contracts away from feast/famine.
- **Pain:** "I deliver client work all day. I have no time for prospecting. My pipeline is whoever LinkedIn surfaces."
- **Goal:** A steady drip of decision-maker conversations while delivering on retainers.
- **Channels:** LinkedIn (their own posts), referrals, podcasts.
- **Buying trigger:** Just finished a contract and pipeline is empty.
- **Objection:** "I don't want to look automated — my brand is human."
- **What wins them:** "Approve every message before it sends" + the ability to write in their actual voice via Knowledge Base.

**The unifying psychograph across all three:** *founder-as-operator, allergic to corporate sales tooling, values privacy, willing to pay for premium if it saves their time.*

## 3. Anti-ICP — who we deliberately don't chase

Premium positioning is choosing what *not* to do. These segments are off-limits in 1.0:

| Segment | Why we say no |
|---|---|
| **Coaches / course creators** | Wrong channel (Instagram/TikTok over LinkedIn), wrong deal size ($500–$3k can't justify outbound ROI), saturated by guru-tech, brand-association risk. |
| **E-commerce / D2C** | LinkedIn isn't where their buyers live. Wrong tool entirely. |
| **Enterprise sales teams (SDR-led orgs)** | They have Outreach.io, Salesloft, Apollo. Influentia is built for solo operators, not 5-person SDR teams. We'll lose feature wars and burn support. |
| **Recruiters** | Different motion (sourcing not selling), LinkedIn sees their automation patterns differently, account-ban risk is double. |
| **Real estate / financial advisors** | Heavily regulated DM compliance, wrong network for our automation stance. |
| **Anyone wanting "1,000 messages a day"** | Our caps are conservative on purpose. If they push back, decline. They'll get banned and blame us. |

If a prospect from one of these segments emails: **don't sell.** Reply with one sentence ("Influentia's built for B2B founders selling services or software — we wouldn't be the right fit for you, sorry") and move on.

## 4. Positioning statement (canonical)

> **For** B2B founders, agency owners, and consultants
> **who** are tired of being their own SDR and don't trust cloud-IP outreach tools,
> **Influentia** is a local-first LinkedIn + Reddit pipeline autopilot
> **that** runs on their machine, in their voice, with privacy intact —
> **unlike** Expandi, Phantombuster, and Lemlist
> **which** route everything through shared cloud IPs that LinkedIn now actively detects and bans.

This is not landing copy. It's the *internal compass* that landing copy is derived from.

## 5. Messaging architecture

### 5.1 Headline tier (hero, ads, OG)
- **Primary:** "LinkedIn outreach on autopilot. On your machine. In your voice."
- **Alt 1:** "A daily pipeline of warm B2B leads — without hiring an SDR."
- **Alt 2:** "Local-first outreach. Built for solo founders, not sales teams."

(Note: avoid leading with "built by an agency" in the hero — it confuses the brand. Use that line on the About page or in objection-handlers, not in the primary positioning.)

### 5.2 Sub-headline tier
- "Influentia finds your ideal clients, reads what they post about, writes personalised messages, follows up automatically — and never sends your data anywhere."
- "$97/month. Cancel anytime. Built for solo founders, not sales teams."

### 5.3 Value-prop trio (use on landing, not all together)

| Prop | Sentence | Best paired with |
|---|---|---|
| **Privacy** | "Your LinkedIn cookie never leaves your computer." | Privacy-paranoid SaaS founders. |
| **Personalisation** | "Every message reads the prospect's actual posts. No templates." | Consultants who care about voice. |
| **Outcome** | "Your first booked call in seven days, or full refund." | Agency owners who want proof. |

### 5.4 Objection handlers (canonical answers — use verbatim in FAQ, sales replies, ads)

- *"Will my LinkedIn account get banned?"*
  → "Influentia runs on your machine, on your IP, with conservative human-like caps (15 connections, 20 messages, 5 comments per day). LinkedIn sees a normal session. No tool can guarantee zero risk, but cloud-IP tools are the ones LinkedIn detects fastest — and that's the category we deliberately reject."

- *"How is this different from Expandi / Phantombuster / Lemlist?"*
  → "They run from cloud IPs. We run on yours. They scale to enterprise SDR teams. We build for solo founders. They charge $99 for a checkbox-feature war. We charge $97 because we built it inside an agency that uses it daily."

- *"What if I'm not technical?"*
  → "Download, double-click, log into LinkedIn the normal way. The dashboard walks you through your first scan. If you can use Stripe and Calendly, you can use Influentia."

- *"$97 is a lot. Can I get a discount?"*
  → "It's the price of one bad cold-call agency hour. If you don't book a qualified call in your first 14 days, we refund every cent." (Then mean it.)

- *"Can I run it for my whole team?"*
  → "Not yet. Influentia 1.0 is one user, one machine. Pro tier with team seats ships in Q3. If you need that today, ask about Authentik DFY."

## 6. The ecosystem flywheel — Influentia ↔ Authentik ↔ Content (separate brands, shared mechanic)

Influentia is a standalone SaaS. Authentik is a standalone agency. They share *Ermo* as the operator and Pain Trend data as the content asset, but they keep separate brands, sites, funnels, and pricing. Drawing it out:

```
                    ┌───────────────────────────────┐
                    │   ERMO'S LINKEDIN + NEWSLETTER│
                    │   "Pain Trend Reports"        │
                    │   weekly insights, citing     │
                    │   data from Influentia        │
                    └─────────────┬─────────────────┘
                                  │
           ┌──────────────────────┼──────────────────────┐
           │                      │                      │
           ▼                      ▼                      ▼
   founders read it       agencies cite it       buyers find it
           │                      │                      │
           └──────────┬───────────┴──────────┬───────────┘
                      │                      │
              top of funnel for      top of funnel for
              INFLUENTIA             AUTHENTIK STUDIO
                      │                      │
                      ▼                      ▼
        ┌─────────────────────┐  ┌─────────────────────┐
        │   INFLUENTIA $97    │  │   AUTHENTIK STUDIO  │
        │   self-serve SaaS   │  │   $3,500–10,000/mo  │
        │   own brand         │  │   own brand         │
        │   influentia.io    │  │   ermoegberts.com   │
        └──────────┬──────────┘  └──────────┬──────────┘
                   │                        │
        small upsell card in dashboard:     │
        "Need it done for you?            uses Influentia
         → Authentik partner offer"       internally for clients
                   │                        │
                   ▼                        ▼
            warm referral              real client wins
                                     become case studies
                                       cited in content
                                            │
                                  (loops back to top)
```

**Three product implications for the Influentia dashboard:**

1. **Soft DFY referral card** in Settings — *not* embedded as "Influentia Pro" or implying it's the same brand. Phrasing: "Need this run for you? Authentik Studio (a partner agency we trust) does this as DFY — see if it's a fit." Linked Calendly. Visually a separate-feeling card, not a primary CTA.
2. **Public Pain Trend Report engine** — weekly publication on Ermo's LinkedIn + newsletter. Cited as "data from Influentia," not "from Influentia by Authentik." Influentia gets distribution; Ermo (and Authentik through him) gets thought leadership. The mention is light.
3. **Anonymous opt-in toggle** in Settings — customers consent to their scans contributing to the public reports. Default off. Privacy-first opt-in. This is how the data engine grows without breaking the local-first promise.

**What we deliberately don't do:**
- ❌ Co-brand. No "Influentia by Authentik" wordmark, footer, OG image, or email signature.
- ❌ Cross-sell aggressively. The DFY card is one card, not a sales sequence.
- ❌ Run a single funnel for both businesses. Each gets its own landing, pricing, sales cycle.
- ❌ Imply that paying Influentia gives you any access to Authentik. They're separate transactions.

## 7. Pricing decision (locked)

Influentia tiers are Influentia's pricing. Authentik DFY is a separate business with its own pricing — listed here only as the upsell exit ramp, *not* as an Influentia tier.

| Brand | Tier | Price | Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Influentia** | Influentia | **$97/mo** or **$970/yr** (save $194) | All three ICP personas |
| **Influentia** | Influentia Pro _(Q3 2026)_ | $197/mo or $1,970/yr | Agency owners with VAs / consultants with assistants |
| **Authentik Studio** _(separate business)_ | DFY engagement | $3,500–$10,000/mo (custom) | Influentia customers who say "I don't have time" + direct prospects |

Note: Authentik DFY is *not* "Influentia Pro Plus" or "Influentia Enterprise." It's a different product from a different business. The Influentia dashboard surfaces it as a *partner referral*, not as a tier.

**$97 reasoning** (locked):
- Competitive frame: Expandi $99, Lemlist $99, Phantombuster $69 — we sit at the premium end with privacy as the differentiator.
- Margin: leaves ~$20–30/mo headroom per customer for proxied Anthropic API costs even on heavy use.
- Psychology: under-$100 mental barrier; ends-in-7 lift; "expense without approval" zone.
- Anchors annual at clean $970 (compelling save-2-months math).
- Anchors DFY at 50× (mentally walkable). A $19 or $29 price would have anchored DFY at 170×+, which reads as "two unrelated products" rather than "wedge → premium."

**Refund / guarantee policy (locked):**
> "Book your first qualified call within 14 days, or we refund every cent. Then keep using Influentia anyway — we just want you to win."

(That last sentence is the trust-builder. Most companies say "refund." Few say "and keep the product." It costs us nothing — license validates against subscription anyway — and reads as confidence.)

**No legacy pricing to migrate.** Influentia 1.0 is the first paid release; there are no prior paying customers at a different tier. Clean launch.

## 8. The public content engine — Pain Trend Reports

This is the most underexploited asset in the codebase right now. Reddit Signal already produces it. Publishing it is the missing step.

### 8.1 The mechanic
Every week, the system aggregates buyer-intent signals across all customer scans (anonymised, opt-in). Output: "What B2B buyers complained about this week — top 10 pains, surfacing subreddits, sample post excerpts (anonymised)."

### 8.2 The distribution
- **LinkedIn post** every Tuesday, written by Ermo, citing Influentia data.
- **Newsletter** ("Buyer Intent Weekly") on Substack/ConvertKit.
- **Public archive page** on `influentia.io/buyer-intent` — SEO play.
- **OG previews** designed for LinkedIn share — high reshare odds.

### 8.3 The flywheel effect
- LinkedIn readers see Ermo posting weekly with proprietary data → Authentik thought-leader credibility.
- Newsletter readers convert ~3% to Influentia trials over 90 days.
- Public archive ranks for "B2B founder pain points 2026," etc. → SEO traffic.
- Customers see their tool generating public insight → reinforces "this is built by an agency, not a script."

### 8.4 What needs to ship to make this real
- An anonymisation pipeline in the worker (scrub names, employers, identifying details).
- An opt-in toggle in the dashboard ("Share my anonymised signals to power public reports — defaults to off").
- A simple `/buyer-intent` page on the landing site.
- A scheduled task that compiles + emails the report each Monday.

This is post-relaunch work, but the dashboard's Settings should already have the opt-in toggle from day one.

## 9. Brand voice (canonical)

Influentia has its own voice. Authentik has its own voice. They overlap because Ermo writes both, but each speaks from its own position. Influentia speaks as a product; Authentik speaks as a service.

Influentia voice is:
- **Operator, not vendor.** "Made for founders who hate being the SDR."
- **Direct, not slick.** Short sentences. Real claims. No "transform your business."
- **Confident, not aggressive.** We push back on bad fits, we don't chase.
- **Privacy-aware, not paranoid.** State the facts; don't fearmonger.
- **Honest about risk.** LinkedIn ToS, account safety, trade-offs — all covered upfront.

Examples (use these, copy them verbatim):
- ✅ "Local-first outreach. Built for solo founders, not sales teams."
- ✅ "Cloud-IP outreach tools are the ones LinkedIn detects fastest. We deliberately built the opposite."
- ✅ "Most outreach tools optimise for SDR teams. We optimise for the founder who hates being the SDR."
- ✅ (About page only, once) "Built by the team behind Authentik Studio. We use this every day for our own clients."
- ❌ "Influentia by Authentik Studio — your premium B2B growth partner."  *(merges brands; weakens Influentia)*
- ❌ "Revolutionary AI-powered platform for next-gen B2B revenue acceleration."
- ❌ "10x your pipeline overnight!"

If a piece of copy could be on a generic SaaS landing page, it's wrong for Influentia. If it sounds like agency marketing, it's wrong too — that's Authentik's job, not Influentia's.

## 10. The one-line tests (for any positioning decision)

1. **ICP test:** which of the three personas is this for? If "all three" or "none of the three" — sharpen.
2. **Wedge test:** does this reinforce local-first / privacy / agency-built? Or does it sound like generic SaaS?
3. **Flywheel test:** does this feed Authentik or content? Or is it a feature for a feature's sake?
4. **Premium test:** would a $97/mo customer feel this matches their price point? Or does it feel like a $19 tool?
5. **Decline test:** would I be comfortable saying "you're not the right fit" to a coach asking for this?
